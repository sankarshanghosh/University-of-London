you tell it at the start what you want the world to have in it. Say you want a box at the top, and a floor
at the bottom for the box to bounce on. You simply form the physics engine of this
in the setup function of your p5 sketch. Then, inside our draw loop, all you
do is ask it where is the floor now? Where is the box now?
Where is the floor now? Where is the box now? The engine responds by
giving you some numbers. The location and the rotation
of these objects in the world. And you then have to draw them. All the complicated calculations have
been taken care of inside this black box. Your only job is to ask for updates and to draw the object where
the engine tells you to. This is a very important point to make. A physics engine is simply a library
that you feed numbers into and it responds by giving you more numbers. You have to use p5 drawing tools in order to give life to the numbers
the physics engine returns to you. There many physics engines out there. Here's a selected list. All of them are based on the same paradigm
